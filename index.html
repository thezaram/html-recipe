Barbecue Chicken Grilled Pizza

This quick and easy barbecue chicken grilled pizza recipe is for those summer cookouts. I often make it as an appetizer while the ribs are cooking. Use whatever toppings you normally like on regular pizza, but the barbecue chicken is always a crowd-pleaser! The dough never makes a perfect rectangle, but we have fun guessing what it looks like ("That looks like America!") . For the chicken, I use pre-cooked grilled chicken from the store.

Recipe Information

prep time: 15 minutes

cook time: 10 minutes

Additional time: 5 minutes

total time: 30 minutes

servings: 6

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

For 6 servings

  • 1 cup chopped cooked chicken
  • Half cup barbecue sauce, divided
  • 1 (13.8 ounce) package refrigerated pizza dough
  • 2 Tablespoons olive oil, or as needed
  • 1 cup shredded Colby-Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes, or to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the grill for medium heat and lightly oil the grate.
  2. Combine chicken and 2 tablespoons barbecue sauce in medium bowl until evenly coated.
  3. Roll out dough onto a work surface, cut in half to form 2 rectangles. Brush tops of dough with olive oil.
  4. Place dough, oil-sides down, onto preheated grill, cook until bottom is browned and top is bubbling up, about 5 minutes.Brush tops of dough with oil, flip
  5. Spread remaining barbecue saucs over crusts, top with chicken, cheese and tomatoes
  6. Cover the grill, cook until cheese is melted, 2 to 3 minutes. cool before slicing, 2 to 3 minutes

Tips

Make sure that oven or grill is properly preheated before cooking the pizza for best results.

If you're a lover of a beautiful cheese pull, let the pizza cool for a few minutes before slicing to allow the cheese to set slightly.
